Interior renovation of your home or apartment is a significant investment that requires careful financial planning. Without a realistic budget, you risk running out of funds mid-project or compromising on quality. As professionals with experience in over 100 projects in Tirana and Durrës, we've helped many clients plan their budgets effectively.

## What Does Interior Renovation Include?

Before discussing numbers, let's understand what work is typically involved: structural works (demolition, new gypsum walls), technical installations (electrical, plumbing, HVAC), finishes ([plastering](/en/wall-plastering/), [painting](/en/painting/), tiling, flooring), and furnishing.

### Budget Allocation Rule of Thumb

- **30-35%**  -  Technical installations (electrical + plumbing)
- **25-30%**  -  [Gypsum works](/en/gypsum-works/), plastering and painting
- **20-25%**  -  Tiles, flooring, doors
- **10-15%**  -  Contingency (always keep a reserve!)

## 7 Tips to Save Without Losing Quality

1. **Don't skimp on hidden installations**  -  cheap cables and pipes inside walls are a recipe for expensive repairs later
2. **Invest in quality gypsum**  -  Knauf/Rigips panels cost slightly more but last significantly longer
3. **Plan lighting from the start**  -  changes after gypsum is closed are expensive
4. **Do everything at once**  -  phased renovation costs more overall
5. **Get multiple quotes**  -  compare at least 3, but don't automatically choose the cheapest
6. **Keep 10-15% contingency**  -  there are always surprises
7. **Prioritize by impact**  -  safety first, then insulation, then visual impact
